Dragon

Dragons are legendary creatures, typically with serpentine or reptilian traits, featured in the myths of many cultures. They are often depicted as large, winged, fire-breathing creatures with a long tail and sharp claws.

Origins

Dragons appear in the folklore of many cultures worldwide. In European traditions, dragons are often portrayed as evil creatures that hoard treasure and are slain by heroes. In contrast, Eastern cultures, such as Chinese and Japanese, often view dragons as benevolent and wise beings associated with water and weather.

Characteristics

  • Large, serpentine body
  • Wings and the ability to fly
  • Fire-breathing capabilities
  • Sharp claws and teeth
  • Often associated with treasure hoards
