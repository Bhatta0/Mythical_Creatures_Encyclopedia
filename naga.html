Naga

Nagas are serpent-like beings in Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain mythology. They are often associated with water, fertility, and protection.

Origins

Nagas are mentioned in ancient Indian texts such as the Mahabharata and the Puranas. They are considered guardians of treasures and sacred places.

Characteristics

  • Serpent-like appearance
  • Associated with water and fertility
  • Guardians of treasures
  • Often depicted with multiple heads
  • Can take human form
