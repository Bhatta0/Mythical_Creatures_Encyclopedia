Yali

Yali is a mythical creature in Indian art and architecture, often depicted as a lion-like beast with elements of other animals such as elephants and horses.

Origins

Yali is commonly found in South Indian temple architecture, symbolizing power and protection. It is often used as a decorative motif.

Characteristics

  • Lion-like appearance
  • Combines features of elephants, horses, and other animals
  • Symbol of power and protection
  • Common in South Indian temple architecture
  • Often depicted with a fierce expression
