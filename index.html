Mythical Creatures

Dragons are legendary creatures, typically with serpentine or reptilian traits, featured in the myths of many cultures.

The phoenix is a long-lived bird that cyclically regenerates or is otherwise born again.

A unicorn is a legendary creature that has been described since antiquity as a beast with a single large, pointed, spiraling horn projecting from its forehead.

Garuda is a legendary bird-like creature from Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain mythology, often depicted as the mount of Lord Vishnu.

Nagas are serpent-like beings from Hindu and Buddhist mythology, often associated with water, fertility, and protection.

Yali is a mythical creature from Indian art and architecture, often depicted as a lion-like beast with elements of other animals.
