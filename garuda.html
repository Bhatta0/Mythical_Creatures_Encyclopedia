Garuda

Garuda is a divine bird-like creature in Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain mythology. He is the mount of Lord Vishnu and is known for his immense strength and speed.

Origins

Garuda is prominently featured in Hindu scriptures such as the Mahabharata and the Puranas. He is often depicted as a protector against evil forces.

Characteristics

  • Half-man, half-bird appearance
  • Mount of Lord Vishnu
  • Symbol of strength and speed
  • Enemy of serpents (Nagas)
  • Often depicted with golden wings
